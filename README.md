## What Is BiBBOS?

BiBBOS is a sovereign business operating system compressed into a single self-contained HTML file. Every customer, invoice, product, employee, journal entry, and project intention your business generates lives on your device — not on a server you do not control.

The file contains its own UI, its own logic engine, its own accounting engine, its own payroll engine, its own ML intelligence layer, a 78-slide embedded knowledge module, a Document Vault, a Formation View across 22 domains, and six stewardship cultivation environments. When you download it, you own it completely. No expiry. No cloud dependency. No API calls.

> *The vessel is the record. There is no reconciliation because there was never a separation between the operation and the data.*

## The Six Stewardship Cultivation Environments

> *The vessel does not tell the steward what to do. It makes the present condition continuously visible and then waits. The steward decides.*

These are not features. They are developmental environments — each one addresses a specific weakness in human observation that reconciliation architecture systematically produces.

| # | Environment | What it cultivates |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Condition Age** | Duration visibility — a 47-day overdraft is different from a fresh one |
| 2 | **Metric Trajectory** | Directional awareness — TT$-1.1M falling is different from TT$-1.1M stable |
| 3 | **Formation View** | Whole-vessel observation — 22 domains visible simultaneously, fixed viewport |
| 4 | **Observation Prompts** | Noticing avoidance — domains with conditions not visited in 3+ days |
| 5 | **Intention-Action Visibility** | Accountability — stated intentions alongside what the vessel actually records |
| 6 | **Sovereign Reflection** | Session mirror — screens visited, actions taken, what persists unresolved |

Every tile shows a live signal — open AR amount, days since backup, overdue invoices, model count, peer status. Conditions carry their age. Every domain shows when the steward last looked at it.

### Vessel Awareness
On every boarding the vessel acknowledges the steward:
> *"Welcome back. The vessel has been waiting 3 days. 1 condition requiring attention."*

Time-of-day greeting. Days since last session. Active condition count. Dismisses after 6 seconds. Click to dismiss immediately.

### Intention-Action Visibility (Intention Thread)
Each intention shown as a two-column thread — **Intended** vs **Vessel Shows** — without interpretation:
- Left: budget, target date, sponsor, PM, rationale
- Right: timeline days remaining/overdue, recorded spend from JEs, linked invoices
- Gap is visible. The vessel never explains it. The steward reads the meaning.

### Sovereign Reflection
Accessible from the Log panel `◎ Reflect` button:
- Screens observed this session (clickable chips)
- Actions taken (success log entries)
- Conditions encountered (warnings/errors)
- Documents generated (vault entries)
- What persists — current unresolved conditions with age and → View links

### Formation Hub Architecture
- WebSocket hub at `ws://[LAN-IP]:8080/formation` — any browser, any device on the network
- Compact registry — only named fields stored per vessel (never full payloads)
- Backpressure guard — `safeSend()` checks `ws.bufferedAmount` before every write; 512KB cap
- Rate limiting — 80 outbound msg/s per client, 30 non-heartbeat inbound msg/10s per sender
- Memory confirmed stable — heap 9–11MB across 21 simultaneous vessels
